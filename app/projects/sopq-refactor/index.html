sale template refactoring

two words: lighter. templates.

overview.

Originally when I wrote the first sale templates we started sending back in 2015, there was a goal to reduce file-size and introduce some new techniques. Then we had this glorious idea to start using background-images. This started as a novel gesture, but blew up into our everyday go-to...which was not the intention.

I started using our testing platform, Litmus, to run some diagnostic tests and noticed that these templates were HUGE. Completely and unnecessarily HUGE. So from that moment, after I presented to Justin and co, we decided to try a test template. I stripped just about everything from the original and built a new one from scratch.

Bad.

the redesign.

I approached this one with the idea of just stripping it down. We tested 2 versions over the course of the year. One of the tests, the first one, was more of an on-the-fly test that just happened.

Good.

Then later in the year I took it a step further and really broke the template down into pieces and rebuilt it much cleaner and much more functional. The final result was roughly 300-500k in total file load reduction which led to a dramatically quicker open, an increased open rate, and an increased CTR, in the end.

Better.
